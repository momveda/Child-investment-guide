Part 2

Before You Invest — Protect Your Family First

Most parents jump straight into investments. Get the order right, and everything else becomes easier to build on.

1
Health InsuranceA medical emergency shouldn't derail your child's future fund.
2
Term InsurancePure protection — ensures your goals continue even if you aren't there.
3
Emergency Fund6–12 months of expenses, so you never have to break an investment mid-way.
4
Child InvestmentsNow you're investing on a stable foundation, not a shaky one.

Part 6

Mistakes That Quietly Cost Parents the Most

  • Waiting to start "until things settle down"
  • Putting everything into just PPF or just SSY
  • Ignoring education inflation when setting goals
  • Stopping SIPs during market corrections
  • Buying insurance products as investments (ULIPs, endowment plans)
  • Never increasing the investment amount year over year
  • Chasing the latest "hot" investment trend
Part 7

If My Baby Was Born Today, Here's What I'd Do

1
Buy adequate family health insurance
2
Buy term insurance — 10–15x annual income
3
Build a 6–12 month emergency fund
4
Start a monthly index fund SIP immediately
5
Open an SSY account if I have a daughter
6
Use PPF for stable, tax-efficient fixed returns
7

Increase every investment by 10% every year
