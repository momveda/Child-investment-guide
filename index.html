Child Investment Guide

A Simple Guide to Investing for Your Child

A practical framework to balance growth, safety, and peace of mind for your child’s future.

You don’t need to be a financial expert to start investing for your child.

This guide simplifies the most effective options so you can make confident decisions early.

How to use this guide

  1. Read through each investment option
  2. Pick a mix based on your comfort (growth vs safety)
  3. Start small and stay consistent

1. Mutual Funds (Best for Growth)

Why choose this:

  • Highest potential to beat inflation
  • Ideal for large education corpus
  • Flexible monthly investment (SIP)

Key benefits:

  • Power of compounding over 10–15 years
  • Disciplined investing
  • Liquidity when needed

Returns & Tax:

  • Returns: 10% – 14% (long-term average)
  • Child funds: can deliver ~12–20% in strong cycles
  • Tax: 10% LTCG (above ₹1 lakh), 15% STCG

Top 3 Funds

  • SBI Children’s Fund: High growth (~23–25%)
  • ICICI Child Care Fund: Balanced & stable (~15–19%)
  • Tata Young Citizens Fund: Consistent performer (~12–17%)

Pro Tip: Increase SIP by 10% every year—this can significantly boost your final corpus.

2. Public Provident Fund (PPF)

Why choose this:

  • Government-backed safety
  • Guaranteed returns
  • Tax-efficient

Key benefits:

  • Zero risk investment
  • Stable long-term wealth building
  • Disciplined savings habit

Returns & Tax:

  • Interest: ~7.1% – 7.5%
  • Tax: Fully tax-free (EEE)

Pro Tip: Use PPF as your safety anchor while mutual funds drive growth.

3. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)

Why choose this:

  • High safe returns
  • Designed for girl child
  • Goal-based investment

Key benefits:

  • Higher returns than PPF
  • Fully tax-free
  • Long-term compounding

Returns & Tax:

  • Interest: ~8.0% – 8.5%
  • Tax: Fully tax-free

Pro Tip: Invest more in early years—compounding continues even if contributions stop later.

Recommended Investment Mix

  • Mutual Funds: 50–60% (Growth)
  • PPF: 20–25% (Stability)
  • SSY: 15–25% (Goal-based)

Start early, stay consistent, and review once a year.

This guide is a starting point.
Adapt it as your child grows and your life evolves.
